Frequently asked questions

1. What is this?

This website contains an interactive modeling program used to describe how the future might unfold based on the userâ€™s inputs. The key focus is Artificial Intelligence, but some questions and outputs also take into account human intelligence enhancement and extinction risk.

Most of the questions in this model do not have answers widely agreed upon in the scientific community. We challenge users to think and examine their own assumptions when they input the modeling data. Then, the output shows some of the implications of these assumptions. Users can go back and input different data and get a different output. The goal is to experiment, have fun, and learn something about future possibilities.

2. Who are you?

We are a research team that built this project over Summer 2008 using a research grant provided by the Singularity Institute for Artificial Intelligence. Anna Salamon led a team consisting of Steve Rayhawk, Tom McCabe, Michael Anissimov, Bryan Bishop, and Rolf Nelson. Additional consultation was provided by Michael Vassar.

3. Why are most of the inputs probability distributions?

Because reality is uncertain, and many of the questions have answers that no one knows exactly. The focus of this model is to embrace uncertainty, rather than sweep it under the rug, as is done by futurists who set rigid timelines.

4. What determined how you selected the user's guide arguments?

We tried our best to pick arguments that summarize the major positions held by thinkers on these topics, and to achieve balance by presenting a range of views. Including every view held by everyone who has ever thought on these matters is impossible, so we tried to limit the arguments for each question to fit on one or two pages.
